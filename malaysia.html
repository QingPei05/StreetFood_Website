Malaysia Street Food

Char Kway Teow

Wok-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, eggs, and chives — smoky and savoury.

Asam Laksa

Tangy tamarind fish broth, thick rice noodles, mint, pineapple, torch ginger.

Oyster Omelette

Fresh oysters with eggs and a light starch batter, pan‑fried to crisp edges and a soft center.

Nyonya Laksa

Rich coconut curry noodle soup with prawns, fish cake, and fragrant herbs.

Cendol

Shaved ice, coconut milk, gula melaka, and green jelly — iconic Malaccan dessert.

Chicken Rice Balls

Soft ping‑pong sized rice balls, served with steamed/roasted chicken and sweet chili sauce.

Satay Kajang

Char‑grilled skewers with smoky aroma, served with classic peanut sauce.

Yong Tau Foo

Tofu and vegetables stuffed with fish paste or minced meat, served in soup or with sauce.

Mee Rebus

Yellow noodles in a thick gravy of sweet potato, spices, and aromatic herbs.

Otak‑otak

Spiced fish paste wrapped in leaves and grilled over hot coals.

Ikan Bakar (Perlis)

Fresh fish marinated in spices, grilled to perfection, served with tangy sambal belacan.

Pulut Mempelam

Glutinous rice topped with sweet ripe mango, drizzled with creamy coconut milk.

Nasi Ulam (Kedah)

Steamed rice tossed with chopped herbs, vegetables, and toasted coconut.

Pekasam

Fermented freshwater fish delicacy seasoned with salt, toasted rice, and spices.

Rendang Minang

Slow‑cooked beef in coconut milk and spices — rich and aromatic.

Lemang

Glutinous rice cooked in bamboo with coconut milk; festive favourite.

Serunding

Finely shredded meat cooked with spices and coconut — a popular side/snack.

Nasi Kerabu

Blue‑tinted herb rice with raw salad, grilled fish/chicken, and budu.

Akok

Soft, custardy kuih with caramelised edges — coconut & palm sugar aroma.

Keropok Lekor

Chewy fish sausage fritters, best hot with chili sauce by seaside stalls.

Satar

Cone‑shaped banana leaf parcels with spiced fish and coconut, grilled over charcoal.

Ipoh Hor Fun

Silky flat rice noodles in delicate chicken‑prawn broth — Ipoh’s comfort classic.

Salt Baked Chicken

Whole chicken marinated with Chinese herbs and baked in coarse salt — Ipoh signature.

Ipoh White Coffee

Smooth, aromatic coffee roasted with margarine and served with condensed milk.

Puding Diraja

Royal dessert with bananas, raisins, dates and creamy milk-egg sauce — once served to Pahang royalty.

Gulai Tempoyak Ikan Patin

Pahang’s signature river catfish in fermented durian curry — tangy, creamy and aromatic.

Hinava

Fresh fish cured with lime, ginger, chilies and shallots — bright and zesty.

Mee Goreng Tuaran

Stir‑fried handmade egg noodles with vegetables, char siu and egg — springy texture.

Kolo Mee

Sarawak Laksa

Kek Lapis Sarawak
